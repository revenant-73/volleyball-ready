JUMP READY PWA — PROJECT DESCRIPTION + TODO LIST
For: Century High School Volleyball + Tualatin Valley Volleyball Club
Build tool: ZenCoder in VS Code
Project type: Simple no-login Progressive Web App (PWA)
Primary user: Volleyball athletes in the gym
MVP constraint: QR code → readiness check → suggested track → session cards → exercise videos


============================================================
1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
============================================================

Build a mobile-first Progressive Web App called "Jump Ready."

Jump Ready is a simple no-login gym tool for volleyball athletes. Athletes scan a QR code at the gym, open the app on their phone, complete a quick readiness check, receive a suggested training track, and then follow a 60-minute volleyball strength/movement session using session cards and exercise demo videos.

The program is based on three training layers:

1. Structural Capacity
   - Can the athlete absorb, hold, and tolerate force?
   - Focus: strength, range, tissue capacity, landing control, joint tolerance.

2. Neuromuscular Coordination
   - Can the athlete organize strength into adaptable movement?
   - Focus: balance, rhythm, coordination, crawling, reaching, deceleration, reactive movement.

3. Force Expression Under Speed
   - Can the athlete express force quickly and repeat it?
   - Focus: jumping, sprinting, bounding, pogos, elastic/reactive actions.

The app should support athlete autonomy. The app may suggest a track, but athletes can override the suggestion.

The three training tracks are:

A. Build
   - For newer, sore, inconsistent, or lower-readiness athletes.
   - More controlled strength, isometrics, lower impact, longer rest.
   - Main idea: Own the position before you speed it up.

B. Organize
   - For athletes who need coordination, rhythm, balance, and movement quality.
   - More crawling, reaching, balance, deceleration, partner/reactive movement.
   - Main idea: Solve movement problems, not just complete reps.

C. Express
   - For fresh, experienced athletes ready for power and speed.
   - More explosive jumps, sprints, bounds, and low-volume high-intent work.
   - Main idea: Fast, crisp, explosive — stop before quality drops.


============================================================
2. MVP GOALS
============================================================

The MVP should do only these things well:

1. Open from a QR code on any phone.
2. Work well on mobile screens.
3. Let athletes complete a readiness check.
4. Suggest Build, Organize, or Express based on readiness answers.
5. Allow athletes to override the suggested track.
6. Show a complete 60-minute training session.
7. Show the correct exercise variation for the chosen track.
8. Include video placeholders or embedded demo videos for each exercise.
9. Include an exercise library organized by movement family.
10. Include safety rules and simple coaching cues.
11. Be installable as a PWA.
12. Work without user accounts.
13. Require no database for the MVP.

Do NOT build these in MVP:
- Login
- Athlete accounts
- Coach dashboard
- Attendance tracking
- Analytics
- Saved history
- Payments
- Team assignments
- Complex admin tools

PAGE 8: Safety Rules
Purpose:
- Simple safety guardrails.

Content:

Pain Rule:
- 0–2/10 pain: okay, monitor.
- 3–4/10 pain: regress or change exercise.
- 5+/10 pain: stop that movement.

Landing Rule:
- If landings are loud, collapsing, or unstable:
  - reduce jump height
  - reduce reps
  - switch to Build or Organize

Soreness Rule:
- If sore:
  - no max jumping
  - more isometrics
  - more range/control
  - more crawling and mobility

Effort Rule:
- Build: 6–8/10 effort
- Organize: 6–7/10 effort
- Express: 9–10/10 intent, low volume

Core message:
"Your job is not to survive the workout. Your job is to choose the right challenge."


PAGE 9: About the Program
Purpose:
- Explain the program simply.

Content:
"Jump Ready helps volleyball athletes build the frame, organize the system, and express power. The goal is not perfect technique or grinding through soreness. The goal is to become more durable, explosive, coordinated, and adaptable for the actual demands of volleyball."

7. EXERCISE DATABASE — STARTER CONTENT
============================================================

Create these movement families and exercises.

WARM-UP / MOVEMENT PREP
These are track = all.

1. Bear Crawl Forward/Backward
- Sets/reps: 2 x 20 seconds
- Cue: Move quietly and keep the floor close.
- Easier: Hold bear crawl position.
- Harder: Add direction changes.

2. Deep Squat Rocks
- Sets/reps: 1–2 x 30 seconds
- Cue: Find range without forcing it.
- Easier: Hold onto support.
- Harder: Add reach or rotation.

3. Lateral Lunge Flow
- Sets/reps: 1–2 x 5 each side
- Cue: Shift smoothly from side to side.
- Easier: Reduce depth.
- Harder: Add reach.

4. Pogos
- Sets/reps: 2 x 15 seconds
- Cue: Bounce quietly and quickly.
- Easier: Two-foot ankle rocks.
- Harder: Faster contacts.

5. Scap Push-Ups
- Sets/reps: 2 x 8
- Cue: Push the floor away.
- Easier: Hands elevated.
- Harder: Slower tempo.


STRUCTURAL CAPACITY: KNEE / QUAD / SPLIT SQUAT

Build:
Split Squat Iso Hold
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20–30 seconds each leg
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Description: Hold a split squat position with control.
- Cue: Stay tall and own the position.
- Easier: Reduce depth or use hand support.
- Harder: Go deeper or hold longer.

Organize:
ATG Split Squat with Reach
- Sets/reps: 3 x 5 each leg
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Description: Move through a comfortable split squat range while reaching in different directions.
- Cue: Reach long and stay balanced.
- Easier: Shorter range.
- Harder: Add more reach directions.

Express:
Split Squat Switch Jump
- Sets/reps: 3 x 4–6 each leg
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Description: Quick split squat jumps with controlled landings.
- Cue: Switch fast, land quiet.
- Easier: Step-switch without jumping.
- Harder: Jump higher while staying crisp.


STRUCTURAL CAPACITY: CALF / ACHILLES / ANKLE

Build:
Standing Calf Raise Iso Hold
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20–30 seconds
- Rest: 30 seconds
- Cue: Push through the big toe and stay tall.
- Easier: Two legs.
- Harder: Single leg.

Organize:
Single-Leg Calf Raise with Balance Reach
- Sets/reps: 3 x 6 each side
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Cue: Reach without losing the foot tripod.
- Easier: Use wall support.
- Harder: Reach farther or in multiple directions.

Express:
Pogo Jumps
- Sets/reps: 3 x 15–25 seconds
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Quick off the floor, quiet on the floor.
- Easier: Smaller bounces.
- Harder: Higher/faster pogos.


STRUCTURAL CAPACITY: HAMSTRING / HIP / POSTERIOR CHAIN

Build:
Glute Bridge Hold
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20–30 seconds
- Rest: 30 seconds
- Cue: Push the floor away and keep ribs down.
- Easier: Two-leg bridge.
- Harder: Single-leg bridge.

Organize:
Single-Leg RDL Reach
- Sets/reps: 3 x 5 each side
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Cue: Reach long and keep balance.
- Easier: Tap the back foot down.
- Harder: Reach to multiple targets.

Express:
Broad Jump to Stick
- Sets/reps: 3 x 3
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Jump far, land quiet, freeze.
- Easier: Smaller jump.
- Harder: Add a second jump after the stick.


STRUCTURAL CAPACITY: TRUNK / BRACE / ROTATE

Build:
Dead Bug
- Sets/reps: 3 x 6 each side
- Rest: 30 seconds
- Cue: Move slow without letting your back arch.
- Easier: Move arms only or legs only.
- Harder: Longer reach.

Organize:
Bear Crawl Shoulder Tap
- Sets/reps: 3 x 8–12 taps
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Cue: Tap without rocking.
- Easier: Wider feet.
- Harder: Narrower feet or slower taps.

Express:
Plank to Sprint Start
- Sets/reps: 3 x 3–5 reps
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Pop up and win the first step.
- Easier: Step up instead of pop up.
- Harder: Add coach clap start.


COORDINATION: LANDING / DECELERATION

Build:
Snap-Down to Stick
- Sets/reps: 3 x 4
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Cue: Land like the floor is ice.
- Easier: Smaller snap-down.
- Harder: Add arm swing.

Organize:
Jump-Land-Turn
- Sets/reps: 3 x 4
- Rest: 45 seconds
- Cue: Find the floor, then find the next direction.
- Easier: Turn after landing.
- Harder: Coach calls direction while athlete is in the air.

Express:
Lateral Bound to Stick
- Sets/reps: 3 x 4 each side
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Push hard, land clean.
- Easier: Smaller bound.
- Harder: Bound farther or react to a call.


COORDINATION: LATERAL MOVEMENT

Build:
Low Lateral Step + Pause
- Sets/reps: 3 x 5 each way
- Rest: 30 seconds
- Cue: Stay low and balanced.
- Easier: Smaller step.
- Harder: Longer pause or deeper position.

Organize:
Partner Mirror Shuffle
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20–30 seconds
- Rest: 45 seconds
- Cue: Read the partner, not your feet.
- Easier: Slow leader.
- Harder: Faster direction changes.

Express:
Shuffle → Sprint → Stick
- Sets/reps: 4–6 reps
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Change gears fast, then stop clean.
- Easier: Shorter distance.
- Harder: Add reactive direction call.


COORDINATION: GROUND-BASED MOVEMENT

Build:
Bear Crawl Hold
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20 seconds
- Rest: 30 seconds
- Cue: Knees hover, back quiet.
- Easier: Knees down.
- Harder: Longer hold.

Organize:
Bear Crawl Direction Changes
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20–30 seconds
- Rest: 45 seconds
- Cue: Move forward, back, and sideways without rushing.
- Easier: Fewer direction changes.
- Harder: Coach-called direction changes.

Express:
Crawl → Pop-Up → Sprint
- Sets/reps: 3–5 reps
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Smooth transition, fast first step.
- Easier: Crawl to stand.
- Harder: React to coach clap.


COORDINATION: SHOULDER / SCAP / UPPER BODY

Build:
Push-Up Hold
- Sets/reps: 3 x 20 seconds
- Rest: 30 seconds
- Cue: Push the floor away.
- Easier: Hands elevated.
- Harder: Lower hold.

Organize:
Scap Push-Up + Reach
- Sets/reps: 3 x 6–8
- Rest: 30–45 seconds
- Cue: Reach without collapsing.
- Easier: Hands elevated.
- Harder: Add alternating reaches.

Express:
Explosive Incline Push-Up
- Sets/reps: 3 x 4–6
- Rest: 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Fast hands, solid body.
- Easier: Higher incline.
- Harder: Lower incline.


FORCE EXPRESSION: POWER CIRCUIT

Build:
Power Circuit — Build
- Sets/reps: 3 rounds
- Content:
  - 3 approach footwork patterns, no jump
  - 3 snap-down landings
  - 10-second calf iso
  - Rest 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Rhythm first, then control.
- Easier: Walk-through speed.
- Harder: Faster approach rhythm.

Organize:
Power Circuit — Organize
- Sets/reps: 3 rounds
- Content:
  - 2 approach jumps
  - 2 block jumps
  - 1 lateral shuffle to stick each direction
  - Rest 60 seconds
- Cue: Jump, land, and be ready for the next task.
- Easier: Lower jumps.
- Harder: Add coach direction call.

Express:
Power Circuit — Express
- Sets/reps: 4 rounds
- Content:
  - 3 max approach jumps
  - 3 block jumps
  - 1 short sprint, 5–10 yards
  - Rest 60–90 seconds
- Cue: Max intent, clean landings, no junk reps.
- Easier: Reduce to 2 jumps each.
- Harder: Add target touch or timed sprint.


RESET / MOBILITY
These are track = all.

1. 90/90 Breathing
- Sets/reps: 1 x 60 seconds
- Cue: Long exhale, ribs down.
- Easier: Feet on floor.
- Harder: Feet elevated.

2. Deep Squat Breathing
- Sets/reps: 1 x 45–60 seconds
- Cue: Breathe into the position.
- Easier: Hold onto support.
- Harder: Add side-to-side rocks.

3. Hip Flexor Stretch
- Sets/reps: 1 x 30 seconds each side
- Cue: Tall posture, quiet ribs.
- Easier: Shorter stance.
- Harder: Add reach.

4. Calf Stretch
- Sets/reps: 1 x 30 seconds each side
- Cue: Keep the heel heavy.
- Easier: Less angle.
- Harder: Bend and straighten knee.

8. READINESS LOGIC DETAIL
============================================================

Create a function recommendTrack(answers: ReadinessAnswers): TrackRecommendation

Suggested scoring approach:

Initialize score:
buildScore = 0
organizeScore = 0
expressScore = 0

Pain:
if pain === "yes":
  buildScore += 5

Soreness:
none: expressScore += 2
mild: organizeScore += 2, expressScore += 1
moderate: buildScore += 2, organizeScore += 2
high: buildScore += 5

Energy:
low: buildScore += 3
okay: organizeScore += 3
high: expressScore += 3

Sessions this week:
0: expressScore += 1, organizeScore += 1
1: organizeScore += 2
2+: buildScore += 2, organizeScore += 2

Trained yesterday:
yes: buildScore += 2, organizeScore += 2
no: expressScore += 1

Decision:
- If pain yes, always recommend Build.
- Else choose highest score.
- If tie, choose safer option in this order:
  Build over Organize over Express when fatigue risk is present.
  Organize over Express when uncertain.

Return:
- track
- reason string

Example reasons:
Build:
"Build is the better choice today because your body is asking for more control, lower impact, and better positions."

Organize:
"Organize is the better choice today because you are ready to move, but today is probably better for rhythm, balance, and clean coordination than max output."

Express:
"Express is the better choice today because you are fresh, pain-free, and ready for higher-intent jumping and speed work."

12. SESSION BUILDER LOGIC
============================================================

Create a function buildSession(track: Track)

It should:
1. Pull warm-up exercises where track = all and section = warm-up.
2. Pull one exercise per movement family for the selected track.
3. Pull the selected power circuit for the selected track.
4. Pull reset exercises where track = all and section = reset.
5. Return an ordered session array grouped by section.

Order:
1. Warm-Up / Movement Prep
2. Knee / Quad / Split Squat
3. Calf / Achilles / Ankle
4. Hamstring / Hip / Posterior Chain
5. Trunk / Brace / Rotate
6. Landing / Deceleration
7. Lateral Movement
8. Ground-Based Movement
9. Shoulder / Scap / Upper Body
10. Power Circuit
11. Reset / Mobility

Important:
- Do not randomly assign exercises in MVP.
- Keep the program predictable and easy to teach.
- Randomization can be added later.

15. PROJECT PRINCIPLES
============================================================

Keep these principles visible during development:

1. Athletes should not need an explanation from a coach to use the app.
2. The app should reduce confusion, not add more instructions.
3. Choice is part of the design.
4. The readiness check guides the athlete, but does not control them.
5. The app should help athletes notice, adapt, and commit.
6. Videos should be short and practical.
7. Every screen should answer: "What do I do next?"
8. Do not build complexity before the simple gym tool works.
9. Make the MVP boringly useful.
10. The first real test is whether athletes actually use it in the gym.

16.3 JUMP VOLUME CAPS
============================================================

Add jump volume guidance to the Safety Rules page and to the Session Builder logic.

Purpose:
- Keep power work from turning into conditioning.
- Reduce unnecessary tendon and joint stress.
- Protect athletes who want to overdo Express work.

Suggested jump caps per session:

Build:
- 0–10 total jumps
- Emphasis: low impact, landing control, approach rhythm, isometrics

Organize:
- 10–25 total jumps
- Emphasis: timing, coordination, landing variability, rhythm

Express:
- 25–45 total jumps max
- Emphasis: high intent, full recovery, crisp execution

Important:
These numbers do not need to be displayed as strict math to the athlete every time, but the app should use them to guide session design.

Add this rule clearly:
"If jump height, landing quality, or intent drops, stop the jump set."

Add this safety line:
"Power work should end while athletes still look powerful."

Optional app feature:
- Show a small jump estimate on the Session Overview page.
- Example:
  "Estimated jumps today: 18–24"
  "Track: Organize"


============================================================
16.4 WEEKLY PROGRESSION SETTING
============================================================

Add a simple week selector or program phase setting.

Purpose:
- Allow the same session structure to progress across the summer.
- Keep the program from feeling random.
- Keep development simple while still giving the training plan direction.

Suggested phases:

Weeks 1–2: Learn + Control
Goal:
- Learn the tracks.
- Learn the movement families.
- Learn how to self-select honestly.
- Keep jump volume lower.
- Emphasize Build and Organize.

App behavior:
- Lower volume.
- More conservative Express recommendations.
- More cues about landing quality and control.

Weeks 3–5: Build + Coordinate
Goal:
- Increase strength, range, rhythm, and adaptable movement.
- Add more reach, direction changes, partner constraints, and moderate jump volume.

App behavior:
- Slightly higher volume.
- More Organize options.
- More partner/reactive constraints.
- Express available when readiness is high.

Weeks 6–7: Express + Refine
Goal:
- Increase speed and power while keeping movement quality high.
- More approach jumps, block jumps, bounds, and sprint starts.
- Still allow Build and Organize for sore athletes.

App behavior:
- Express track can include higher intent work.
- Maintain jump volume caps.
- More emphasis on full rest and crisp execution.

Week 8: Test + Celebrate
Goal:
- Calibrate progress.
- Let athletes see what adapted.
- Keep testing useful, not stressful.

Possible tests:
- Approach jump
- Broad jump
- Repeated approach jumps: best and average of 5
- Pogo rhythm
- Single-leg balance reach
- Split squat control/range

App behavior:
- Add optional testing cards.
- Avoid framing testing as a ranking system.
- Include this message:
  "Testing tells us what adapted. It does not define who you are."
